Activity Pattern

Summary of Data from Activity Track in Last Two Weeks



The activities I encounter in my daily life can be devided into four categories:
Random activity, class time, daily routine, and habit. In the long-term (weekly)
pattern, random activity occupies the largest proportion, taking up 49.4% of time
in a week. Class time and daily routine share the similar proportion (each share
19.3% and 20.6%), taking up about 2/5 of the day. Habit, such as using phone, has
the smallest proportion, which takes up 10.7%, about 1/5 of time in a week.

The daily pattern, likewise, have the similar arrangement as the weekly pattern.
From the example of Sep 18, random activity have a more significant difference
compared to daily routine and class time. The difference between habit is not significant.

Before doing the activity tracking, I thought the activity that take up the most in my daily life were daily routine and phone, which often frustrated me as I thought my time in the past were wasted a lot on something meaningless. However, after looking closely on what I was actually doing, I found that it was rather I lacks a specific goal and plan for my time arragement, that caused the time I spent on each activity extended and became unproductive. This situation can be blamed on my own personality as being sluggish and overly cautious during making process, further influencing my mood and taking more time for me to get back into regular emotional state.

There are some problem I've discovered

  • Lack of specific goal and management
  • Overly cautious; Thinks a lot, does a little
  • Not proficient on skills-based tasks
  • Feels like "I'm not ready" to do tasks

Being overly cautious and skill issues caused myself to overthink and did less;
Lack of specific goals and feeling of "not ready" reduce motivation and attentio
n neccessiated to work on the task consistenetly. All of them can result in
unproductive working process, creating stress and fatigues that make the time
management even worse.
