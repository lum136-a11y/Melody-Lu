What's The Main Point That Time Management Isn't Effective?

-- Consider How Much Time Is Actually Needed

As I got the feedback from ULC, I learned that it was impossible to multitask
-- which I misunderstood as "having a good time-management." A better
time management involves a careful consideration of how much time is acutally
needed, according to the situation and individual condition. For example,
I often stressed when I rushed to finish the assignment in a limited time.
To avoid this, I should assign more time for myself, so I can concentrate more.

A More Concrete Step:

Consider my mental health and current situation, I found that
the appropriate amount of time assign for work is about
4 - 6 hours per day. It's also better to have preparation
before starting the work. It will save more time and avoid
burnout in the long run.
