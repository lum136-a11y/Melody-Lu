Analysis: The activities I encounter in my daily life can be devided
into four categories: Random activity, class time, daily routine,
and habit. In the long-term (weekly) pattern, random activity
occupies the largest proportion, taking up 49.4% of time in a week.
Class time and daily routine share the similar proportion (each share 19.3% and 20.6%),
taking up about 2/5 of the day. Habit, such as using phone, has the smallest
proportion, which takes up 10.7%, about 1/5 of time in a week.

The daily pattern, likewise, have the similar arrangement as
the weekly pattern. From the example of Sep 18, random activity
have a more significant difference compared to daily routine and
class time. The difference between habit is not significant.

Interpretation: According to the pie charts above,
the large proportion of random activity indicates that
I spent the most of the time on things that are not
planned or expected. The numeral difference of random
also infers that there're many variations involved in.
Class time and daily routine are repeatitive and more
predictable, which explains the moderate proportions
they have taken in. They're relatively stable as they

meaning that I spent the least time on them, and they
do exist repeatitively.

Reflection: Before doing the activity tracking, I thought
the activity that take up the most in my daily life were
daily routine and phone, which often frustrated me as I thought
my time in the past were wasted a lot on something meaningless.
However, after looking closely on what I was actually doing,
I found that it was rather I lacks a specific goal and plan for
my time arragement, that caused the time I spent on each activity
extended and became unproductive. This situation can be blamed on
my own personality as being sluggish and overly cautious during making
process, further influencing my mood and taking more time for me to
get back into regular emotional state.

  • Lack of specific goal and management

  • Not proficient on skills-based tasks

use of time. Being overly cautious and skill issues caused myself to

All of them can result in unproductive working process, creating stress
and fatigues that make the time management even worse.
